The conversation around artificial intelligence has fundamentally shifted. A year ago, your attendees might have been satisfied with a keynote explaining "What is a Large Language Model?" Today, they're asking, "How do we integrate LLMs to drive revenue?" or "What's our strategy for responsible AI implementation before we fall behind?" The demand isn't for futurist predictions anymore; it's for practical, actionable guidance from people who are actively building, deploying, and shaping the AI-powered world.

As a conference director, your challenge is to cut through the deafening noise of AI hype and find the signal. You need speakers who don't just talk about transformation but who can provide a roadmap for it. They need to be more than just technologists; they must be strategists, ethicists, and communicators who can connect with a diverse business audience. The following leaders are precisely that—the voices shaping tomorrow's business landscape right now, offering the specific insights your audience is hungry for.

## The Architects of Modern AI

Some speakers don't just comment on the AI revolution; they started it. Bringing one of these architects to your stage lends an unparalleled level of authority and perspective to your event.

### Adam Cheyer
As the co-founder of Siri, Adam Cheyer has fundamentally changed how billions of people interact with technology. His work didn't stop there; he went on to co-found Viv (acquired by Samsung to become Bixby) and Sentient. Adam isn't just a historian of the conversational AI he helped create; he's a forward-looking visionary. He provides audiences with a rare, first-hand account of building world-changing technology, the lessons learned from navigating massive tech acquisitions, and a clear vision for the future of ambient computing. **His keynote is perfect for an audience that needs to understand where human-AI interaction is truly headed, beyond the current chatbot craze.**

### Peter Norvig
Few people can claim to have written the book on AI—Peter Norvig literally did. His textbook, *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach*, is the standard in the field. As the former Director of Research at Google, he has been at the epicenter of AI's most significant breakthroughs for decades. Peter excels at demystifying the core principles of AI, grounding today's generative marvels in the foundational science that makes them possible. Booking Peter is about providing your audience with deep, foundational knowledge. He's the ideal speaker for events that want to move beyond surface-level trends and equip attendees with a lasting, intellectual framework for understanding AI's trajectory.

## The Enterprise Strategists & Implementers

For most businesses, the AI challenge isn't invention; it's adoption. These speakers are masters at bridging the gap between AI potential and organizational reality, offering frameworks for strategy, leadership, and ROI.

### Mark Chamness
After a distinguished career leading AI and data initiatives at IBM Watson, Mark Chamness brings an invaluable "from the trenches" perspective on enterprise AI. He has seen firsthand why some AI projects deliver transformative value while others wither on the vine. Mark's sessions are a masterclass in AI strategy, focusing on how to build data-driven cultures, identify high-ROI use cases, and manage the organizational change required for success. **He's the speaker for your C-suite and leadership audience, answering the critical question: "How do we make AI a core competency of our business?"**

### Katie McMahon
As a leader who has taken AI companies like SoundHound from startup to public offering, Katie McMahon offers a potent mix of tech savvy and business acumen. She specializes in the practicalities of building and monetizing AI products, particularly in the complex world of voice AI and data platforms. Katie’s insights are crucial for any organization trying to turn an AI feature into a profitable product line. She speaks with authority on go-to-market strategy, competitive differentiation, and building sustainable business models around cutting-edge technology.

### Vishal Sharma
Building a successful AI product requires more than a great algorithm; it requires exceptional technical leadership. With extensive experience leading AI/ML teams at Google, Vishal Sharma specializes in the art and science of scaling AI initiatives. He addresses the challenges that keep engineering VPs and product leaders up at night: How do you attract and retain top AI talent? How do you structure teams for rapid innovation? What technical infrastructure is needed to support enterprise-grade AI? Vishal provides a practical playbook for building high-performing AI organizations from the ground up.

## The Human-Centric Futurists

AI is not just a story about technology; it's a story about people. These speakers focus on the profound impact AI is having on work, society, and the very way we think, ensuring your audience considers the most important part of the equation: the human element.

### Gary Bolles
The phrase "future of work" is everywhere, but Gary Bolles provides the substance behind the buzzword. As the Chair for the Future of Work for Singularity University, he offers a clear-eyed perspective on how AI is reshaping jobs, skills, and careers. Gary moves beyond the dystopian "robots will take our jobs" narrative to offer a more nuanced and actionable vision. He equips leaders with strategies for reskilling their workforce, fostering lifelong learning, and designing organizations that can thrive amidst constant technological change. **His keynote is essential for any conference focused on HR, leadership, or organizational development.**

### Dan Russell
Dan Russell, Google's "search anthropologist," offers a fascinating and critical perspective: how do real people *actually* use and understand complex systems like AI? He has spent his career studying the intersection of human cognition and technology, and his insights are gold for anyone building AI-powered products or services. Dan teaches audiences how to look beyond the code to understand user behavior, cognitive biases, and the keys to designing AI that is truly intuitive and helpful. He ensures the "user" is at the center of the AI conversation.

### Russell Wald
As the former Director of Policy for the Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI (HAI), Russell Wald is a leading voice on one of the most critical topics in the field: responsible AI. He is an expert at navigating the complex ethical, policy, and governance challenges that come with deploying powerful AI systems. Russell's sessions are not abstract philosophical debates; they are practical discussions about building trust, mitigating bias, and creating frameworks for safe and ethical AI use. For any organization serious about its long-term reputation and social license to operate, his message is not just important—it's non-negotiable.

## The Innovators and Groundbreakers

These speakers are on the front lines, applying AI in novel ways to create new products, disrupt markets, and redefine what's possible. They bring an infectious energy and a playbook for real-world innovation.

### Robert Daigle
Working at the vanguard of Google DeepMind, Robert Daigle is focused on one of AI's most exciting frontiers: AI for Science. He demonstrates how generative AI is accelerating discovery in fields like medicine and materials science, offering a stunning glimpse into a future where AI is a collaborative partner in solving humanity's biggest challenges. Robert is brilliant at translating this cutting-edge research into tangible business opportunities, showing how the same principles can be applied to drive R&D and innovation within any industry.

### Gopi Kallayil
As the Chief Brand Evangelist at Google, Gopi Kallayil has a unique vantage point on how AI is transforming marketing, customer experience, and brand strategy. He brings stories from one of the world's most innovative companies to show how AI can be used to create hyper-personalized customer journeys, unlock new creative potential, and build deeper, more meaningful connections with audiences. Gopi’s keynotes are dynamic and inspiring, perfect for sales, marketing, and innovation teams looking for a competitive edge.

### Mo Tiwari
As a Harvard-educated entrepreneur and the CEO of a generative AI company, Mo Tiwari represents the new wave of leaders building the future in real time. He bridges the academic and the practical, explaining the core concepts behind generative AI while simultaneously sharing his founder's journey of building a product, finding a market, and scaling a business in this fast-moving space. Mo is the ideal speaker for audiences of entrepreneurs, product managers, and investors who want an authentic, current, and unfiltered view of the generative AI startup ecosystem.
